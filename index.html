Orthopaedic Physiotherapy

Orthopaedic Physiotherapy focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of musculoskeletal conditions, including bone, joint, ligament, and soft tissue injuries. It aims to alleviate pain, restore mobility, and improve strength, helping patients recover from surgeries, fractures, arthritis, and other orthopaedic conditions.

Neurological Physiotherapy

Neurological Physiotherapy specializes in the rehabilitation of patients with neurological conditions such as stroke. It focuses on improving movement, strength, balance, and coordination through tailored exercises and therapeutic techniques, helping patients regain independence and enhance their quality of life.

Cardiorespiratory Physiotherapy

Cardiorespiratory Physiotherapy helps improve heart and lung function in patients with conditions like COPD, asthma, or heart disease. Through breathing exercises and strengthening techniques, it enhances lung capacity, circulation, and endurance for better overall health.

Paediatric Physiotherapy

Paediatric Physiotherapy focuses on the assessment and treatment of children with developmental, musculoskeletal, or neurological conditions. It uses age-appropriate exercises and techniques to improve movement, strength, coordination, and flexibility, helping children reach their full physical potential and achieve developmental milestones.
